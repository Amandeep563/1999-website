Toys

Baby's First Blocks

The #1024 block set replaced 1978-1988's #414 Baby's First Blocks. At first, the only difference between the sets was that the #1024 was sold with triangular blocks opposed to the square blocks sold with the #414. In 1997, the set was changed and sold with a new design on the canister (juggling teddy bear opposed to baby stacking blocks) and the blocks were dramatically changed. Opposed to soft hollow plastic blocks, the 1997+ blocks have recessed sides with molded designs on the inside wall. The blocks can be stacked or dropped into the canister through the shapes in the removable lid. Measures 7-1/2" high, 5-7/16" diameter. Designed for children ages 6 months to 2 years old.

PocketStation

The PocketStation is a memory card peripheral by Sony Interactive Entertainment for the PlayStation home video game console.[3] It was released in Japan in 1999. The device acted not only as a memory card, but was interactive itself via a small monochrome LCD display and buttons on its case. Many PlayStation games included software that could be downloaded and played on the PocketStation
